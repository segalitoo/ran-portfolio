Brand Refresh — Startup Co

A complete visual identity overhaul for a Series A fintech startup looking to mature their brand as they scaled from 20 to 200 employees.

Overview

Startup Co needed a brand identity that could grow with them. The project encompassed a new logo system, color palette, typography guidelines, and a comprehensive brand book for internal and external use.

Challenge

The existing brand felt scrappy and inconsistent across touchpoints. The team had outgrown their original DIY identity but needed to retain the approachability that their customers loved.

Solution

I developed a flexible identity system built around a refined wordmark and a modular icon library. The new palette balanced professionalism with warmth, and the brand book gave every team a clear framework to work within.

Results

Brand recognition scores increased by 40% within six months of launch. The sales team reported that prospects perceived the company as more established, contributing to a 25% improvement in close rates.

Mobile App Design — HealthTrack

End-to-end UX and UI design for a health and wellness app that helps users build sustainable daily habits through gentle nudges and progress tracking.

Overview

HealthTrack approached me to design their iOS and Android app from the ground up. The product focuses on hydration, sleep, movement, and mindfulness — four pillars their research showed mattered most to their target audience of busy professionals.

Challenge

The wellness app market is saturated with products that overwhelm users with data. HealthTrack needed to feel calming rather than clinical, and the design had to encourage daily engagement without relying on guilt or pressure.

Solution

I designed a minimal interface centered around a single daily view with soft animations and encouraging micro-copy. Progress is shown through organic visual metaphors rather than harsh charts, and notifications were designed to feel like a friend checking in.

Results

The app launched with a 4.8-star rating on the App Store. Daily active retention after 30 days was 62%, well above the industry average of 25% for health apps. Users frequently cited the "non-judgmental" design in reviews.

Website Redesign — Artisan Bakery

A warm, inviting website redesign for a family-owned bakery that wanted their online presence to feel as welcoming as walking through their front door.

Overview

Artisan Bakery had been using a template website for five years that no longer reflected the quality of their craft. I redesigned their site to showcase their story, daily menu, catering services, and online ordering — all with the handmade character their brand deserves.

Challenge

The bakery's customers skew older and less tech-savvy, so the site needed to be dead simple to navigate. At the same time, the owners wanted to highlight their sourcing philosophy and the artistry behind each product without overwhelming visitors.

Solution

I built a clean, image-forward layout with large typography and intuitive navigation. The daily menu updates through a simple CMS the owners can manage themselves. Warm textures and hand-drawn accents give the site a tactile, analog quality.

Results

Online orders increased by 150% in the first quarter after launch. The average session duration doubled, and the bakery received multiple comments from customers saying the new site "feels just like the shop." Catering inquiries rose by 80%.

Dashboard UI — DataFlow

A data visualization dashboard for a B2B analytics platform, designed to make complex datasets accessible to non-technical marketing teams.

Overview

DataFlow provides marketing analytics to mid-size e-commerce companies. I was brought in to redesign their core dashboard experience, focusing on clarity, scannability, and the ability to surface actionable insights without requiring a data science background.

Challenge

The existing dashboard tried to show everything at once, resulting in cognitive overload. Users were exporting data to spreadsheets because they couldn't find what they needed in the product itself — defeating the purpose of the tool.

Solution

I restructured the information architecture around user goals rather than data types. A new "insights feed" surfaces the most important changes automatically, and customizable widget layouts let each team member build their own view. Data visualization was simplified with consistent chart patterns and plain-language annotations.

Results

CSV exports dropped by 70%, indicating users were finding answers within the product. The NPS score for the dashboard rose from 18 to 52. Three enterprise clients cited the redesign as a key factor in renewing their annual contracts.

Packaging Design — Bloom Tea

Packaging and visual identity for a premium loose-leaf tea brand launching with six signature blends at specialty grocery retailers nationwide.

Overview

Bloom Tea was a new direct-to-consumer and retail tea brand founded by two former sommeliers. I designed their full packaging system — tins, pouches, gift sets, and shipping materials — along with the supporting visual identity for their launch collection.

Challenge

The premium tea market is crowded with brands that either lean too traditional or too minimalist. Bloom Tea needed packaging that felt elevated and gift-worthy while still communicating the unique flavor profile of each blend at a glance on a retail shelf.

Solution

I created a botanical illustration system where each blend has its own hand-drawn ingredient motif rendered in a signature color. The tins use a matte finish with foil-stamped details, and a consistent layout structure ties the collection together while letting each blend express its own personality.

Results

Bloom Tea sold out their initial retail run within three weeks. The packaging was featured in Print Magazine's annual design review and helped the brand secure placement in 120 additional stores within six months of launch.

Campaign Visuals — Summit Festival

A multi-channel visual campaign for an outdoor music and arts festival, spanning digital ads, social media content, posters, and on-site wayfinding.

Overview

Summit Festival is a three-day music and arts event held annually in the Pacific Northwest. I was hired to create the complete visual campaign for their 2025 edition, covering everything from the first announcement posts to the physical signage attendees encounter on-site.

Challenge

The festival needed a visual system flexible enough to work across Instagram stories, large-format posters, wristbands, and stage banners — all while maintaining a cohesive identity. The lineup announcement had to generate excitement in a staged rollout over eight weeks.

Solution

I designed a modular visual system built around layered topographic patterns and a bold typographic hierarchy. The color palette shifts subtly across campaign phases to signal progression from early announcements to the event itself. Templates were delivered in Figma so the in-house team could generate day-to-day social content independently.

Results

The campaign drove a 35% increase in early-bird ticket sales compared to the previous year. Social media engagement during the rollout period grew by 200%, and the festival reported that attendees consistently praised the on-site signage for being both beautiful and easy to follow.
